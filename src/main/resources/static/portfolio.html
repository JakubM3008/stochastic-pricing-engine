[PORTFOLIO COVARIANCE DIVERSIFICATION DETAILS]
When asset returns are positively correlated (ρ > 0), price shocks reinforce each other, inflating the expected shortfall volatility (portfolio variance). Conversely, when correlations are low or negative, independent fluctuations offset each other, yielding a diversification benefit ($SD_{uncorrelated} - SD_{correlated}$) mapped via the Cholesky factor matrix: Y = L * Z.
